About Me

Building things that actually work

I'm a third-year BCA-AIML student at UPES Dehradun. I care about building AI systems end-to-end — from training models to deploying them in usable products.

Background

A bit about me

I started coding seriously in my first year of BCA when I built a small automation script for something mundane. That led to Python, then machine learning, then building actual products. I've shipped two production AI systems so far — a face recognition attendance platform and an AI plant care app — and I'm looking for internships where I can do more of that.

What I find most interesting is the space between research and product — taking a state-of-the-art model and making it work reliably in a real environment with real users. That's the hard part, and also the most satisfying.

I'm targeting roles in AI/ML Engineering, Computer Vision, and Full-Stack AI development. I prefer working on things that solve actual problems over things that just look impressive in demos.

How I Think

What I value when building

Ship first, refine after

A working prototype beats a perfect spec every time. Build it, test it, fix what's broken.

Research-backed choices

I pick tools and models because of evidence, not hype. ArcFace over simpler methods because the accuracy gap is real.

End-to-end ownership

I don't hand off to someone else once the model is trained. I deploy it, build the UI, and fix what breaks in production.

Always learning

The field moves fast. I keep up through certifications, side projects, and reading actual papers, not just summaries.
